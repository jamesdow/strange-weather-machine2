## Fault Lines Carried From Stage 1

## Read of the Creator Field

Primark's creator authority is not concentrated in one glamorous fashion elite. It sits across practical scouts: store walkers, haul explainers, family-budget validators, adaptive-fashion advocates, home trend translators and "is it worth the trip?" shoppers. The strongest voices do not simply show clothes. They reduce risk. They answer: will this fit, will it look better than the price suggests, is it worth the queue, is it in store, is the quality real, and can I feel clever rather than cheap for buying it?

The most useful scouts for Primark are therefore not mass-market influencers with generic reach. They are creators who already behave like unofficial infrastructure: they scan stores, decode drops, test styling, narrate availability, name friction, and make value feel socially legible.

What the evidence does NOT show: the evidence does not show a single unified Primark creator community with one shared aesthetic, one hashtag language, or one audience segment. It shows several overlapping authority economies: value fashion, store logistics, family life, adaptive access, home decor, haul culture critique and budget planning.

## Strategic Implications for Stage 2 Synthesis

1. Primark's highest-value creator is a risk-reducer. Creators who can say "I checked the sizing," "here is when to go," "this is worth it," or "this did not work" are more useful than creators who only generate appetite.

2. The store creator is an access mediator. The store is Primark's core advantage and biggest source of ordeal. Creator intelligence should include people who understand store timing, queues, deliveries, local stock and route planning.

3. "Honest review" language matters more than luxury styling language. Stage 1 found that audiences want clever relief. The creator evidence says clever relief is built through caveats, proof and practical evaluation.

4. Adaptive and inclusive creators carry real authority, but only when the work stays specific. Victoria Jenkins and Sophie Morgan are proof-rich because they connect product design to lived access needs. Generic inclusivity content would be weaker.

5. Haul culture is both engine and liability. The same creator format that built Primark discovery is increasingly criticised for overconsumption. Future creator work should shift from "look how much I got" to "look what earned a place."
